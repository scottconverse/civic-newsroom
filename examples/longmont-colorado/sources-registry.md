## TIER A: CORE DAILY/WEEKLY SOURCES

### City of Longmont PrimeGov Portal
- **URL:** https://longmont.primegov.com/public/portal
- **Category:** Civic - Government
- **Update Frequency:** Daily (agendas posted 5-7 days before meetings)
- **Data Type:** Meeting agendas, packets, minutes
- **Check Trigger:** Before each council meeting
- **Confidence:** 0.95
- **Notes:** Primary source for City Council intelligence. If the portal is not directly accessible, search for the specific meeting agenda on the city's main website.

### City of Longmont Official YouTube Channel
- **URL (Livestreams):** https://www.youtube.com/@cityoflongmont/streams
- **URL (All Videos):** https://www.youtube.com/@cityoflongmont/videos
- **Category:** Civic - Government
- **Update Frequency:** After each public meeting (typically within 24 hours)
- **Data Type:** Full video recordings of City Council meetings, study sessions, board/commission meetings, public hearings. YouTube auto-generated transcripts available for all recordings.
- **Check Trigger:** After each council meeting, especially when PrimeGov packets/minutes are not yet published
- **Confidence:** 0.95
- **Notes:** This is the city's official YouTube channel. Video transcripts are Tier A sources — they are machine transcriptions of official government proceedings. Use transcripts to verify: vote counts, council member statements, public comment content, staff presentations, and any claim that would otherwise require meeting minutes. When PrimeGov packets are inaccessible or minutes have not yet been posted, YouTube transcripts serve as the primary grounding source for meeting-based claims. Cite with video title, timestamp, and URL.

## NOTES FROM OPERATION

This registry was built through iterative discovery — each aggregation run surfaced new sources cited in council packets, staff reports, and public comment. The Source Discovery Module (Part 9 of the News Aggregator prompt) was responsible for most Tier B and C additions.

Key lesson: the best sources are often found inside council packets. Staff reports cite engineering firms, planning consultants, state agencies, and data portals that aren't on any city website directory. Follow those citations.
