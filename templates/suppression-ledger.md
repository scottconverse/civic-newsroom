Every story that is killed gets documented here. The suppression ledger serves two purposes: accountability (you can audit what was suppressed and why) and pipeline (suppressed stories are future leads — when missing evidence appears, reopen them).

---

## How to Use This Ledger

1. When a story scores below 6/10 on the confidence rating, or fails the Integrity Checker, it gets suppressed
2. Fill in one entry per suppressed story using the template below
3. Be specific about what's missing — vague reasons don't help future you
4. Always include recommended next steps for making the story publishable
5. Date every entry
6. Review the ledger monthly for stories that can be reopened

- [e.g., "Obtain staff report from City Clerk"]

## Ledger Rules

- Every suppressed story gets an entry — no silent kills
- Be specific about what's missing, not just "needs more reporting"
- Suggest concrete steps to make it publishable
- Do NOT restate rumors or unverified claims in the ledger
- Use neutral topic descriptions (not inflammatory framing)
- Use names only for public figures in public roles
- Date every entry
- Review monthly for stories ready to reopen

## Revision Pathway

Suppressed stories are not dead — they are deferred. To revise and reopen:

1. Address the specific issues listed in the Suppression Ledger entry
2. Re-run the appropriate check (Glitch Check from Prompt 02, Integrity Checker from Prompt 05, or Self-Check from Prompt 08)
3. If all checks pass, update the ledger entry status to "REVISED" with the date
4. Move the story back into the publication pipeline
5. If the story fails revision, update the ledger with new findings and keep status as "SUPPRESSED"
